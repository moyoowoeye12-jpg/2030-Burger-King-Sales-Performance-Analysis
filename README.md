# 2030-Burger-King-Sales-Performance-Analysis
This project provides a comprehensive, data-driven analysis of Burger King’s sales performance for the fiscal year 2030. By leveraging automated data processing and visual analytics, the repository showcases how raw transactional data is transformed into strategic business intelligence.

Introduction


• Objective of the Project: To analyze the annual sales performance of Burger King for the year 2030, specifically evaluating regional contributions, top-    performing product categories, and sales team efficiency. 


• Problem Being Addressed: Identifying which regions and product lines are driving growth versus those requiring operational intervention to meet annual targets. 

• Key Datasets and Methodologies: This analysis utilizes Analytical_Report_2.xlsx and Raw_Data.xlsx. Methodologies include the use of Pivot Tables for data aggregation and radar charts for regional comparisons. 







Story of Data


• Data Source: Internal sales records and point-of-sale (POS) systems captured in Raw_Data.xlsx.

• Data Structure: The data is organized chronologically by month (Jan–Dec) and categorized by region (North, South, East, West), product type, and assigned salesperson. 

• Important Features: Key variables include Revenue (in thousands of USD), Customer Type (Company D, H, BB), and Product Categories (Beverages, Sauces, Jams). 







Data Splitting and Preprocessing


• Data Cleaning: Records from Raw_Data.xlsx were audited for consistency, ensuring that monthly revenue figures matched the regional totals presented in the final dashboard. 

• Industry Context: The analysis is set within the Quick Service Restaurant (QSR) and food retail industry, where high-volume, low-margin transactions require precise logistics. 

• Stakeholders: Senior Management, Regional Operations Leads, and the Supply Chain Department.

• Value to the Industry: Offers a blueprint for "just-in-time" inventory management based on seasonal surges (e.g., June and December peaks). 







Pre-Analysis

• Identify Key Trends: Preliminary review of the Sales Trend line graph shows significant volatility, with notable revenue peaks in June ($55,601.61) and December ($66,642.78). 

• Initial Insights: There appears to be a strong correlation between year-end holiday periods and maximum revenue output, while February represents a significant annual low ($19,985.50). 






In-Analysis

• Analysis Techniques: Using Analytical_Report_2.xlsx, data was segmented using Excel Pivot Tables to determine that Beverages represent the largest product share at 47.84%. 

• Unconfirmed Insights: High performance by Nancy Freehafer (39.30% of sales) suggests a correlation between specific sales territories and individual performance that warrants further investigation.
 
• Recommendations: Investigate the "South" and "North" regions, as the radar chart indicates these are the highest revenue-generating zones. 







Post-Analysis and Insights

• Key Findings:

o Annual High: December achieved the highest monthly revenue of $66,642.78.
 
o Regional Dominance: The North region led all territories with a total of $141,680.34. 

o Customer Loyalty: Company D is the most significant client, accounting for 41.70% of top customer sales.
 
• Comparison: Initial expectations predicted a steady growth curve; however, the data revealed a "W-shaped" recovery throughout the year, with a sharp dip in April and July.





 

Data Visualizations & Charts

• Sales Trend (Line Chart): Tracks the monthly performance from $32.9k in January to the $66.6k peak in December. 

• Product Mix (Donut Chart): Visualizes the dominance of Beverages (47.84%) over Jams/Preserves (22.30%). 

• Regional Performance (Radar Chart): Clearly displays the North ($141k) and South ($93k) as the primary pillars of the business. 








Recommendations and Observations


• Actionable Insights:

o Beverage Expansion: Since Beverages account for nearly half of all sales, introduce premium beverage tiers to increase average transaction value.
 
o Territory Reassignment: Analyze Nancy Freehafer's methods to train Andrew Cencini, who currently holds the smallest share (25.32%) among top salespersons.
 
• Unexpected Outcomes: Despite being a major region, the West ($91,251.98) slightly underperformed compared to the South, despite typically having higher market saturation. 






Conclusion


• Key Learnings: 2030 was a year of extreme seasonality, dominated by Beverage sales and Northern regional performance.

• Limitations: The data in Raw_Data.xlsx does not account for external market factors (e.g., competitor pricing) that may have caused the February and July slumps.
 
• Future Research: Conduct a deep-dive analysis into Company BB to understand why their share (27.13%) lags significantly behind Company D.
